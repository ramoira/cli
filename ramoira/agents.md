# sos — Brand Context

> Consolidating your operation's workflows is not a luxury upgrade — it is the basic precondition for running a hotel without unnecessary chaos.

## Identity

**Brief:** sos: B2B SaaS that consolidates hotel operations workflows into one place so teams can stop chasing and start running.
**Character:** organized  ·  transparent  ·  sophisticated
**Never:** never sound arrogant, neverdownplay competition, never mention we are experts in hotel operations
**Relationship mode:** Things can be better. Here is a small thing that helps.

## Narrative

**Category:** B2B SaaS platform for hotel operations workflow management and consolidation
**Emotional register:** The quiet satisfaction of a shift that ended without a single dropped ball. Not excitement. Not pride. Just the absence of unnecessary friction.
**Meaning clusters:** operational calm — the feeling that nothing is falling through the cracks, team alignment — everyone working from the same information at the same time, practical progress — small, measurable improvements that compound over time, transparency without surveillance — visibility that helps teams, not polices them

## Voice

**Approved tones:** conversational
**Forbidden tones:** overly excited, over-confident, aggressive

**Structural rules:**
- State the operational outcome before the mechanism: what changes for the user before how the product works.
- Use 'we' to refer to sos — never 'sos' in third person in first-person copy contexts.
- Do not open any piece of content with a claim about sos. Open with the user's reality.
- Every paragraph must earn its place by advancing the operational argument, not padding with brand sentiment.
- Active voice. Subject acts. Product enables. Not: 'Efficiency is achieved through sos.'

### Write like this

*product_detail_page*
> Hotel operations run across a lot of moving parts — housekeeping, maintenance, front desk, food and beverage. When those teams are working from different tools, things get missed. sos puts all of it in one place, so your team is always working from the same picture.

### Not like this

*paid_landing_page*
> 🚀 sos is REVOLUTIONISING hotel operations! Industry-leading workflow automation that our team of hospitality experts built specifically to crush your competition. Get started TODAY — limited spots available!
*Why: Violates four absolute constraints simultaneously: claims expertise in hotel operations ('hospitality experts'), uses forbidden word 'revolutionising', uses urgency language ('limited spots'), and uses aggressive competitive framing ('crush your competition'). Exclamation marks and emoji compound the tone failure.*

## Content Tests

Before publishing content, ask:

- Does this content name or remove a real source of operational friction — or is it just describing features?
- Does this content make an operations manager feel more in control, or does it make them feel like they are being pitched to by someone who has never run a shift?
